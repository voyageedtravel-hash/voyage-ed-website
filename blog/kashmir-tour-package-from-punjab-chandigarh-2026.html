Kashmir Tour Package From Punjab & Chandigarh 2026 — Complete 5N6D Guide

A 5-night 6-day trip covers Srinagar's Dal Lake, Gulmarg's meadows, and Pahalgam's pine forests. At Voyage-Ed, Kashmir is our #1 domestic package. This is the complete 2026 guide.

How to Reach Kashmir from Chandigarh

Option 1 — Fly (Recommended)

Fly from Chandigarh Airport (IXC) or Amritsar (ATQ) to Srinagar (SXR). IndiGo and Air India operate this route. Flight time is just 1 hour. This is the fastest and most comfortable option — especially for families and seniors. Return airfares range from ₹4,000–₹9,000 depending on how early you book.

Option 2 — Road (Scenic)

Drive or take a bus: Chandigarh → Jammu (4 hours via NH44) → Srinagar (8–9 hours via Banihal Tunnel, Patnitop, and Jawahar Tunnel). The road journey is spectacular — Patnitop's pine forests and the dramatic Banihal Pass are highlights. Overnight Volvo buses from Chandigarh to Srinagar are available. Total road journey: 12–13 hours.

5 Night 6 Day Kashmir Itinerary

1
Arrival Srinagar — Dal Lake Evening
Arrive Srinagar Airport. Transfer to houseboat or hotel. Afternoon Shikara ride on Dal Lake — the most iconic Kashmir experience. Watch the sunset over the Zabarwan mountains from the lake. Dinner on houseboat. Overnight in Srinagar.
2
Srinagar City — Mughal Gardens + Old City
Morning: Nishat Bagh and Shalimar Bagh — Mughal gardens with Dal Lake backdrop. Visit Shankaracharya Temple for panoramic valley views. Old City: Jama Masjid, Shah-i-Hamdan Mosque, traditional Kashmiri craft markets. Evening: floating vegetable market on Dal Lake (very early morning) if timing allows.
3
Gulmarg — Meadow of Flowers
Drive to Gulmarg (56km, 1.5 hours). One of Asia's highest golf courses. Gondola cable car ride to Kongdori (Phase 1: 2,650m) and optionally Apharwat Peak (Phase 2: 3,980m) — stunning views of Himalayan peaks. Winter: excellent skiing. Summer: wildflower meadows. Return to Srinagar for overnight.
4
Pahalgam — Valley of Shepherds
Drive to Pahalgam (97km, 3 hours) through Awantipora ruins and Avantiswami Temple. Pahalgam: Lidder River valley, Betaab Valley (named after the Bollywood film), Aru Valley meadows, Baisaran (mini-Switzerland). Pony rides available. Overnight in Pahalgam hotel.
5
Sonamarg — Gold Meadow
Drive to Sonamarg (87km from Srinagar, via Manasbal Lake). Thajiwas Glacier accessible by pony (highly recommended). Crystal-clear Sindh River. Snow patches visible even in summer. One of the most scenic drives in all of India. Return to Srinagar. Farewell dinner.
6
Departure Day
Morning shopping — Kashmiri saffron, dry fruits, pashmina shawls, walnut wood crafts. Transfer to Srinagar Airport for flight back. Road travelers start early for Jammu. End of Kashmir holiday.

Best Time to Visit Kashmir from Punjab

  • May–July (Summer): Most popular. Green valleys, wildflowers, pleasant 15–25°C. Gulmarg meadows at best. Tulip garden in April.
  • Sep–Oct (Autumn): Best photography. Chinar trees turn red and gold. Clear skies. Less crowded than summer.
  • Dec–Feb (Winter): Snowfall in Gulmarg. Skiing. Dal Lake may partially freeze. Cold (−5 to 8°C) — carry warm clothes.
  • Aug (Monsoon): Less recommended — rain, occasional landslides on road routes.

Tips from Voyage-Ed — Things Most Blogs Won't Tell You

  • Book houseboats directly: Category A houseboats on Dal Lake are incredible — don't settle for cheaper versions that smell damp. We pre-inspect and only book quality houseboats.
  • Gulmarg Gondola Phase 2: Book Phase 2 tickets online in advance during summer — they sell out. Phase 1 is easily available walk-in.
  • Saffron purchase: Buy only from certified government emporia or directly from Pampore (saffron town, 15km from Srinagar). Market saffron is often adulterated.
  • Weather-proof packing: Even in July, Gulmarg can drop to 5°C. Carry a warm jacket regardless of season.
  • Road vs flight: If budget allows, fly in, drive back — best of both worlds. The road from Kashmir through Patnitop to Jammu is spectacular going south.
Can I visit Kashmir in July–August from Chandigarh?
Yes — July and August are peak summer season. Valleys are lush green. Temperatures are pleasant (15–25°C in Srinagar, cooler in Gulmarg and Pahalgam). Rainfall is moderate. It's one of the most beautiful times to see the valley. The only caution is the Jammu-Srinagar highway which can face occasional disruption during heavy rain — flying is safer in monsoon months.
Do I need any permits or documents for Kashmir?
Indian citizens don't need any special permit for Kashmir. Just your Aadhar card or any government-issued photo ID. For Pahalgam and Sonamarg, your hotel will register your details as per local protocol — this is routine. Foreign nationals need additional documentation but this guide covers Indian travelers from Punjab.
How much spending money should I carry for 6 days in Kashmir?
Over and above package cost: Gulmarg Gondola Phase 2 ₹1,400/person, shopping (saffron, shawls, dry fruits) ₹2,000–₹10,000, pony rides ₹400–₹800, tips ₹500, miscellaneous food/snacks ₹800–₹1,500. Total extra: ₹5,000–₹15,000 per person depending on shopping.
