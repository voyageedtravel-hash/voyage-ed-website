Best Months to Book Delhi to Toronto Flights (Cheapest)

January & February: The Cheapest Window of the Year

January and February are, without question, the cheapest months to fly from Delhi to Toronto. With the holiday rush over and Canadian universities resuming their regular semester, demand dips significantly. Airlines respond by slashing prices, and this is when we regularly see roundtrip fares starting from ₹38,999 to ₹52,000.

This is also an excellent window for Indian students who need to arrive for the winter/spring semester. Student fares during this period often include 2 free checked bags, making the effective savings even larger when you factor in baggage costs.

November: The Hidden Off-Peak Gem

November is frequently underrated as a travel month. Before the Christmas holiday surge kicks in (typically the third week of December), November offers genuine low-season pricing — often in the ₹42,000–₹60,000 roundtrip range. For NRI families visiting relatives in Toronto/Brampton before the holiday rush, November is ideal: lower fares, easier connections, and a quieter Pearson airport.

Early March: Good Value Before Spring Surge

The first two weeks of March typically still carry off-peak pricing before the spring semester break and the onset of warmer travel season push fares upward. Early March departures often come in at ₹45,000–₹62,000 roundtrip, giving you one more window of affordability before fares climb for April and May.

Shoulder Season: Good Value Months

If you have limited flexibility on travel dates but still want to avoid the most expensive peak fares, the shoulder months offer a reasonable middle ground:

  • April and May: Fares typically range ₹52,000–₹75,000 roundtrip. Popular with families and students starting new semesters. Book 60+ days in advance for the best seats at lower prices.
  • September: Post-summer fares begin dropping, often to ₹48,000–₹68,000 roundtrip. Students heading for the fall semester find this window manageable, especially on student fares with extra baggage allowance.
  • October: A solid shoulder month, often ₹50,000–₹72,000. Some airlines run promotional fares during October as they try to fill seats before the Nov–Feb lull.
  • Early December (1st–15th): Before the Christmas spike, early December can offer decent value, ₹52,000–₹70,000 roundtrip. After the 16th, fares jump dramatically.

Peak Season — When to Avoid if You're Budget-Conscious

The summer months of June, July, and August are the most expensive period for Delhi to Toronto flights, for several converging reasons:

  • Indian school and college holidays create enormous outbound demand from India
  • Canadian summer draws NRI families for holidays and reunions
  • Punjabi wedding season in Canada peaks in June–August
  • General summer travel from Europe pushes connecting aircraft to full capacity

During these months, roundtrip fares routinely hit ₹75,000–₹1,05,000 — sometimes higher for last-minute bookings. If you must fly in summer, book 4–5 months ahead (January–February for June travel) and call us to get on our fare alert list.

⚠️
Christmas–New Year: The Most Expensive Period
The Dec 20 – Jan 5 window is the single most expensive period for Delhi–Toronto flights. Expect roundtrip fares of ₹78,000–₹1,20,000+. If you must travel during this period, book at least 4 months in advance and consider flying Dec 18–19 or Jan 6–7 to catch the tail end of lower pricing.

How Far in Advance Should You Book Delhi–Toronto Flights?

The relationship between booking lead time and fare on the Delhi–Toronto route follows a clear pattern:

  • 90–120 days ahead: Best prices. Most seat availability across all cabin classes. This is the sweet spot for budget travellers and students.
  • 60–90 days ahead: Still good prices, especially for economy. Most preferred departure times still available.
  • 30–60 days ahead: Prices begin rising on popular departure times. Economy still bookable at reasonable rates. Premium economy and business prices are set.
  • 14–30 days ahead: Significant price increases on most airlines. Last few economy seats commanding premium pricing. 20–35% more expensive than the 90-day price.
  • Under 14 days: Avoid if possible. Last-minute fares are typically 40–60% above the "fair" price. You're paying for other people's cancellations.

Cheapest Day of the Week to Fly Delhi to Toronto

Day-of-week pricing is real on long-haul routes. Based on our analysis of the DEL–YYZ route:

  • Tuesday and Wednesday departures are consistently 8–16% cheaper than weekend departures on the same week.
  • Friday, Saturday, and Sunday are the most expensive departure days — popular with NRI families and business travellers, so airlines charge a premium.
  • Monday departures can be good value in off-peak months.
  • For connecting flights via Dubai or Doha, the day of the week matters less — Emirates and Qatar Airways offer flat weekly pricing more often than Air India on this route.

Which Route is Cheapest: Non-Stop vs Connecting?

Air India's non-stop DEL–YYZ flight is the only direct option and takes approximately 14–16 hours. Connecting flights via the Middle East (Dubai, Doha) or Europe (London, Frankfurt, Istanbul) typically take 18–24 hours total, but they're often ₹5,000–₹15,000 cheaper on the same dates.

Here's a simple breakdown to help you decide:

  • Choose non-stop (Air India) if: time is critical, you're travelling with young children or elderly relatives, you have heavy baggage, or the fare difference is under ₹8,000.
  • Choose via Middle East (Qatar/Emirates) if: you're a student willing to trade time for savings, you want the best baggage deal, or you enjoy lounge access at Doha/Dubai.
  • Choose via Europe (Air Canada/BA/Turkish) if: you're eligible for a European stopover, you're collecting frequent flyer miles, or you're combining with a Europe visit.

Student Fares: Extra Baggage & Flexibility to Canada

If you're an Indian student flying to a Canadian university or college, standard economy fares are rarely the best deal. Student fares on the Delhi–Toronto route typically offer:

  • Extra free checked baggage: Usually 2–3 bags (23kg each) instead of the standard 1 bag in economy. At ₹4,000–₹7,000 per extra bag, this alone saves ₹8,000–₹21,000.
  • Flexible date changes: Students often need to change their travel dates as exam dates shift or visa appointments move. Student fares usually allow 1–2 free date changes.
  • Lower cancellation fees: If your Canadian study permit is delayed, student fare cancellation policies are far more forgiving than standard economy.
  • Occasionally lower base fares: Some airlines offer 5–15% base fare reductions for students with valid enrollment documentation.
