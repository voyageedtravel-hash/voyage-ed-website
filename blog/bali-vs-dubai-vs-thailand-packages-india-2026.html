Bali — The Romantic Overachiever

Bali is the destination that punches far above its price point. For ₹19,999–₹35,000 per person, Indian travellers get private pool villas, rice terrace breakfasts, cliff-edge restaurants, and some of the most photographed sunsets on earth. No other destination at this price range delivers this level of luxury aesthetic.

🏆
Bali wins for
Honeymoons · Luxury on a budget · Nature lovers · Instagram-worthy travel
Nowhere else gives you a private infinity pool villa for ₹4,000–₹6,000 per night per couple.

What ₹19,999 gets you in Bali (Voyage-Ed package)

  • Return flights from Delhi (with 1 stop via Singapore/KL)
  • 4 nights in a 3-star Kuta/Seminyak hotel with breakfast
  • Airport transfers and local guide
  • Kuta beach, Tanah Lot temple, Ubud market sightseeing

  • Nusa Penida day trip — Kelingking Beach, Angel Billabong
🌴
Bali's hidden advantage

The Indonesian Rupiah is weak against the Indian Rupee right now. ₹1 = approximately 190 IDR. This means food, local transport, spa treatments and attractions are dramatically affordable once you land. A full-body Balinese massage costs ₹400–₹600. A beachfront dinner for two costs ₹800–₹1,400.

Bali's one challenge

There are no direct flights from India to Bali. Most routes connect via Singapore, Kuala Lumpur or Jakarta, adding 2–4 hours of travel time. If you're departing from Amritsar or Chandigarh, account for a Delhi connection too. Total travel time can reach 10–14 hours each way on some routings.

Dubai — The Urban Luxury Capital

Dubai operates in a different category. It is not a beach-and-nature destination — it is a city of superlatives. World's tallest building, world's largest mall, world's fastest roller coasters, world's most photographed skyline. If you want luxury infrastructure, Indian food everywhere, Bollywood-flavoured entertainment, and the feeling of being in a film set — Dubai delivers like nowhere else.

🏆
Dubai wins for
Families · Corporate trips · Luxury seekers · First international trip · Shopping
The best Indian food outside India, world-class safety, and direct flights from 12+ Indian cities.

What ₹22,999 gets you in Dubai (Voyage-Ed package)

  • Return direct flights from Delhi (only 3.5 hrs, Air India/IndiGo/Emirates)
  • 3 nights in a 3-star Deira or Bur Dubai hotel with breakfast
  • Dubai City Tour — Burj Khalifa (124th floor), Dubai Mall, Gold Souk
  • Desert Safari with BBQ dinner, camel ride and cultural show
  • Visa included (pre-arranged, 30-day tourist)

Dubai's premium sweet spot — above ₹55,000

Dubai's true luxury proposition emerges when you spend more. Atlantis The Palm, Burj Al Arab afternoon tea, private yacht cruises, Ferrari World Abu Dhabi, and helicopter rides over the skyline — these experiences are world-class and often surprisingly competitive vs equivalent European luxury.

✈️
Dubai's biggest advantage — flights

Direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Amritsar, Ahmedabad, Hyderabad, Kochi and more. At 3.5 hours from Delhi, Dubai is the easiest international trip for North Indian families who want maximum destination time with minimum travel fatigue.

Dubai's one challenge

The UAE Dirham is tied to the USD and does not weaken. Everything from coffee to taxis to theme parks is priced at international rates. A family of four at Dubai's theme parks (IMG Worlds, Motiongate, or Global Village) can spend ₹25,000–₹40,000 on tickets alone. Budget carefully, especially with children.

Thailand — The Crowd Favourite for Good Reason

Thailand consistently tops the list of most-visited destinations for Indian travellers, and for good reason. It is visa-free, affordable, has extraordinary food (and plenty of Indian restaurants), world-class beaches, iconic city experiences, and enough variety to suit any travel style. Bangkok alone could keep you busy for a week.

🏆
Thailand wins for
Groups · First international trips · Budget travellers · Food lovers · Island hopping
Visa-free entry, most flexible destination, best street food in Asia, iconic for every travel type.

Our Verdict — The Final Word

If you're a couple planning a honeymoon or romantic trip — go to Bali. You will not find better value for a romantic experience anywhere in Asia at this price point. The private villas, the terraced rice fields, the spiritual atmosphere of Ubud — it is genuinely transformative travel.

If you're travelling with family or want maximum comfort with minimum logistics — Dubai is your answer. Direct flights, Indian food at every corner, world-class safety, and enough activities to keep every age group occupied for 5–7 days.

If you're going with a group of friends or on your first-ever international trip — Thailand, no contest. It is the most forgiving destination for inexperienced international travellers, the most entertaining for groups, and the most affordable for those who want to stretch their holiday budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is cheaper — Bali, Dubai or Thailand from India in 2026?
Thailand is consistently the most affordable — packages start from ₹18,999 per person. Bali is next at ₹19,999, while Dubai starts at ₹22,999. However, Dubai offers the most luxury-per-rupee above ₹60,000 budgets. For budget and mid-range travel, Thailand and Bali win. For premium experience and urban luxury, Dubai leads.
Do Indians need a visa for Bali, Dubai or Thailand in 2026?
Thailand is visa-free for Indians up to 30 days. Bali (Indonesia) offers visa on arrival at the airport — costs approximately ₹1,800–₹2,200 and takes 10–20 minutes. Dubai requires a pre-arranged tourist visa costing ₹6,000–₹8,000, applied 3–5 working days in advance. Thailand wins decisively on visa ease.
Which is best for a honeymoon from India — Bali, Dubai or Thailand?
Bali wins for honeymooners overwhelmingly. Private pool villas, rice terrace dinners, cliff-edge restaurants and a deeply romantic atmosphere at far lower cost than equivalent luxury in Dubai. Thailand's Koh Samui and Phuket are also excellent for couples. Dubai suits honeymooners who prefer urban luxury and fine dining over nature.
Which is best for a group trip from India?
Thailand is the most group-friendly — Bangkok nightlife, island hopping, water sports and affordable group accommodation make it ideal for friend groups of 4–12. Bali is great for smaller groups (4–6) seeking villa stays. Dubai works well for corporate groups and family celebrations given its world-class infrastructure.
What is the best time to visit each destination from India?
Bali: April–October (dry season) is best — May and June are excellent right now. Dubai: October–April is ideal; summers (May–September) are extremely hot at 40–48°C. Thailand: November–February is peak season. For travel in May–June, Bali is the best option of the three.
How many days is enough for each destination?
Bali: 5–7 nights minimum (4N Kuta-Ubud or 6N Kuta-Ubud-Nusa Penida). Thailand: 5–7 nights (Bangkok+Pattaya or Phuket only) to 8–10 nights for both. Dubai: 4–5 nights covers key highlights; 6–7 nights to add Abu Dhabi. All three destinations work well in 6 nights from India if planned efficiently.
