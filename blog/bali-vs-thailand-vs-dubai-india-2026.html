Bali vs Thailand vs Dubai — Which is Best for Indians in 2026?

✍ Vishal Sharma, Voyage-Ed Travels📅 2026-06-05⏱ 7 min read

Bali, Thailand, and Dubai are the three most popular international destinations for Indian travelers. Each offers something completely different. At Voyage-Ed, we book all three constantly — here's our honest comparison to help you decide which is right for you in 2026.

Quick Verdict

  • Cheapest overall: Thailand 🇹🇭
  • Best for honeymoon: Bali 🌴
  • Best for families: Dubai 🇦🇪
  • Best for first-timers: Thailand 🇹🇭
  • Best for luxury: Dubai 🇦🇪

Visa Situation — 2026 Update

  • Thailand: Visa-free for Indians — 30 days, no fee, no application. Just passport + return ticket.
  • Bali (Indonesia): Visa on Arrival — $35 USD at Bali airport. Takes 10 minutes. Can extend for another 30 days.
  • Dubai (UAE): Visa on Arrival — free for most Indian passport holders. Stamped on arrival, 30-day stay.

Best For Each Type of Traveler

Couples & Honeymoon → Bali Wins

Bali's rice terraces, volcanic sunrises, private villas, and spa culture make it India's favourite honeymoon destination. Ubud is romantic and cultural. Seminyak and Nusa Dua have luxury beach clubs. Sunset at Tanah Lot temple is an experience no other destination matches.

Families with Kids → Dubai Wins

Dubai is the safest, most organized, and most child-friendly of the three. Dubai Mall, Burj Khalifa, Dubai Frame, Aquaventure Waterpark, and desert safaris — all easily manageable with children. Indian food is everywhere. English is universal. Safety is excellent.

First-Time International Travelers → Thailand Wins

Thailand is the perfect first international trip. Cheapest packages, visa-free, English-friendly, Indian food available, excellent infrastructure. Bangkok's temples, Pattaya's beaches, excellent transport — all at budget prices. Less intimidating than Europe, more exciting than a nearby destination.

Adventure + Nightlife → Thailand Wins

Bangkok's nightlife is world-famous. Pattaya beach + Walking Street. Phuket's island hopping. Koh Samui's Full Moon Party. Thailand packs more experiences per rupee than anywhere else.

Shopping + Luxury → Dubai Wins

Dubai Mall, Mall of the Emirates, and Gold Souk are unmatched for shopping. Tax-free electronics, luxury brands, and gold jewelry are all significantly cheaper than India. For luxury hotels, Dubai's 5-star options are world-class.

Our Recommendation

First international trip: Thailand. Honeymoon: Bali. Family with kids: Dubai. Best value: Thailand. Ultimate luxury: Dubai. All three are fantastic — the "best" one depends entirely on what you're looking for.

Can I combine Bali and Thailand in one trip?
Yes — a popular combination is 3N Bangkok + 2N Pattaya + 3N Bali, totaling 8 nights. You'd fly Bangkok → Denpasar (Bali) directly (2–3 hours, cheap flights). Which has better food — Bali, Thailand or Dubai?
Thailand has the best street food culture — Pad Thai, mango sticky rice, green curry, som tam — all incredibly good and cheap (₹100–₹300 per meal). Bali has excellent food especially in Ubud and Seminyak. Dubai has the best Indian food options (being home to a huge Indian diaspora) and international fine dining, but it's expensive. For food experience: Thailand > Bali > Dubai.
